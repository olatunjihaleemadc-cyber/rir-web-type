# Design & Conversion Principles (All Categories)

A high-converting site comes from deliberate choices about structure, copy, and motion — not a prettier template. These principles apply across PRESENCE, COMMERCE, and PLATFORM, with the category-specific "conversion focus" living in each category's reference file.

---

## What "conversion" means per category
- **PRESENCE** → an enquiry, signup, download, or task completion.
- **COMMERCE** → a completed purchase, booking, or enrolment. The checkout/booking flow is the make-or-break.
- **PLATFORM** → a sign-up that reaches the core "aha" action (activation), then retention.

Design every section to advance the visitor toward the category's specific conversion.

---

## The Conversion Anatomy (public pages)
Each section should answer the question the visitor is silently asking:
1. **"What is this?"** → Hero answers in under 5 seconds. Clear, not clever.
2. **"Is it for me?"** → Subheadline + visual speak to the specific audience.
3. **"Can I trust it?"** → Social proof: logos, testimonials, case studies, ratings, security badges (commerce).
4. **"What do I get?"** → Benefits, not specs.
5. **"How does it work?"** → A simple 2–4 step process removes friction.
6. **"What if…?"** → FAQ kills objections.
7. **"How do I start?"** → A single, obvious primary action, repeated.
If a section doesn't advance this sequence, cut it.

## The Hero Is a Thesis
Open with the single most characteristic thing about the subject. Above the fold the visitor must see **what it is, who it's for, and one primary action.** Avoid the generic template hero unless it's genuinely the best fit.

## One Primary Action, Repeated
Competing actions kill conversion. Pick ONE primary action per page and make it the visual priority; keep secondary actions quieter. Repeat the primary action at the bottom.

## Copywriting — Words Are Design Material
- Write from the visitor's side of the screen; name things by what people recognise.
- **Specific beats clever** — "Delivered in 3 hours across Lagos" > "Lightning-fast delivery."
- **Active voice on buttons** — "Book a call", "Start free", "Add to cart" — not "Submit." Keep the verb consistent through a flow (a "Publish" button → a "Published" toast).
- Benefits over features. Headlines make the offer clear; subheads add the detail.
- Empty states and errors are direction, not mood — say what happened and what to do next.

## Typography & Motion
- Pair a characteristic display face (used with restraint) with a clean body face; set a clear type scale.
- Motion is deliberate, not decorative: hero entrance, scroll reveals, hover micro-interactions. One orchestrated signature moment beats scattered effects. More animation often reads as AI-generated and slows load — and load speed is a conversion factor. Always respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

## Avoid the AI-Generated Look
AI design clusters around three defaults: (1) cream bg + high-contrast serif + terracotta accent; (2) near-black bg + one acid-green/vermilion accent; (3) broadsheet layout with hairline rules. All are legitimate for some briefs but appear regardless of subject. Where the brief leaves a choice free, derive palette and type from the subject's own world instead.

## The Quality Floor (build to it silently)
Responsive to mobile · visible keyboard focus · reduced motion respected · 90+ Lighthouse on public pages · working critical path (form/checkout/signup actually works) · SEO + Open Graph on public pages · fast load.

Critique your own work as you build — screenshot with the Playwright MCP and look with fresh eyes. Before "leaving the house," remove one accessory: cut the one element that doesn't serve the conversion.
